Basic Concepts of Probability Quiz Solution

  1. If you flip a fair coin three times, what is the probability of getting exactly two heads?
    When flipping a fair coin three times, the possible outcomes are 2^3 = 8. The ways to get exactly two heads are HHT, HTH, and THH. So, the probability is 3/8.
  2. If a month is picked at random, what is the probability that the month will start with M?
    The probability that a month will start with M is 1 out of 6 because there are 12 months in a year and only 2 of them start with the letter M (March and May). Therefore, the probability is 1/6.
  3. When you roll a pair of dice, how many outcomes are there?
    When rolling a pair of six-sided dice, there are 36 possible outcomes.
  4. A bag contains 4 red balls and 6 blue balls. What is the probability of drawing a red ball?
    The probability of drawing a red ball is the number of red balls (4) divided by the total number of balls (4 + 6 = 10). So, the probability is 4/10, which simplifies to 2/5.
  5. What is the probability of rolling an even number on a six-sided die?
    The die has 3 even numbers (2, 4, 6) out of a total of 6 possible outcomes (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6). Therefore, the probability is 3/6, which simplifies to 1/2.
  6. A spinner has 8 equal sections, numbered 1 through 8. What is the probability of landing on an even number?
    Half of the sections on the spinner are even numbers (2, 4, 6, 8) out of a total of 8 sections. So, the probability is 4/8, which simplifies to 1/2.
  7. If you draw a card from a standard deck of 52 cards, what is the probability of drawing a spade?
    There are 13 spades in a standard deck of 52 cards. So, the probability of drawing a spade is 13/52, which simplifies to 1/4.
  8. If the probability of an event A is 0.3, what is the probability of the complement of A?
    The complement of an event is 1 minus the probability of the event. In this case, the complement of A is 1 - 0.3 = 0.7.
  9. If you draw two cards from a deck without replacement, what is the probability that both cards are hearts?
    When drawing two cards without replacement, the probability of both events happening is the product of their individual probabilities. The probability of the first card being a heart is 13/52, and the probability of the second card being a heart is 12/51. Multiplying these gives 156/2652, which simplifies to 1/17.
  10. If the probability of rain tomorrow is 0.6 and the probability of not raining is 0.4, what is the probability of either raining or not raining?
    The probability of raining plus the probability of not raining must equal 1.0. So, 0.6 (rain) + 0.4 (not raining) = 1.0.
