Addition Rule Quiz Solution

  1. In a deck of cards, what is the probability of drawing either a spade or a heart?
    The events of drawing a spade and drawing a heart are mutually exclusive since a card cannot be both a spade and a heart. Therefore, you can use the addition rule: P(spade or heart) = P(spade) + P(heart) = 1/4 + 1/4 = 1/2.
  2. A six-sided die is rolled. What is the probability of rolling either a 2 or a 4?
    The events of rolling a 2 and rolling a 4 are mutually exclusive on a fair six-sided die. Therefore, P(2 or 4) = P(2) + P(4) = 1/6 + 1/6 = 1/3. However, since there is no overlap between 2 and 4, you need to divide by 2, resulting in 1/2.
  3. If you flip a fair coin, what is the probability of getting either heads or tails?
    The events of getting heads and getting tails are mutually exclusive outcomes when flipping a fair coin. Therefore, P(heads or tails) = P(heads) + P(tails) = 1/2 + 1/2 = 1.
  4. You draw a card from a standard deck. What is the probability of drawing either a red card or a face card?
    The events of drawing a red card and drawing a face card are not mutually exclusive, as a red face card exists. Therefore, P(red or face card) = P(red) + P(face card) - P(red and face card) = 1/2 + 3/13 - 1/26 = 3/4.
  5. If you randomly select a person from a group, what is the probability of them being either a teenager or an adult?
    The events of being a teenager and being an adult are mutually exclusive categories, covering all possibilities. Therefore, P(teenager or adult) = P(teenager) + P(adult) = 1/2 + 1/2 = 1.
  6. Two six-sided dice are rolled. What is the probability of the sum being either 7 or 11?
    The events of getting a sum of 7 and getting a sum of 11 on two six-sided dice are mutually exclusive. Therefore, P(sum of 7 or 11) = P(sum of 7) + P(sum of 11) = 6/36 + 2/36 = 1/6.
  7. In a bag, there are 5 red marbles and 3 green marbles. What is the probability of drawing either a red marble or a green marble?
    The events of drawing a red marble and drawing a green marble are mutually exclusive. Therefore, P(red or green marble) = P(red marble) + P(green marble) = 5/8.
  8. You roll a fair six-sided die. What is the probability of rolling either an even number or a prime number?
    The events of rolling an even number and rolling a prime number on a fair six-sided die are not mutually exclusive. Therefore, P(even or prime) = P(even) + P(prime) - P(even and prime) = 1/2 + 1/2 - 1/3 = 2/3.
  9. If you spin a spinner with 4 equal sections numbered 1 through 4, what is the probability of landing on either an odd number or an even number?
    The events of landing on an odd number and landing on an even number on the spinner are mutually exclusive. Therefore, P(odd or even) = P(odd) + P(even) = 1/2.
  10. You draw a card from a standard deck. What is the probability of drawing either a black card or a numbered card?
    The events of drawing a black card and drawing a numbered card are not mutually exclusive. Therefore, P(black or numbered card) = P(black card) + P(numbered card) - P(black and numbered card) = 1/2 + 3/4 - 1/4 = 1/3.
